Beginner's Guide

Started in 1977, the original trilogy set off one of the world’s most well-known fictional franchises.
Star Wars became a pop culture phenomenon, changed the way movies were made, and started a merchandising revolution.
Everyboy wanted to wield a lightsaber and use the Force.

Episode IV - A New Hope

When struggling to live his old life behind, Luke Skywalker is forced to move on from Tatooine with Ben Kenobi to larger and harsher reality ending with the destruction of the Empire’s Death Star, a space station capable of destroying planets. On the Star Wars timeline, much happened before it, but this was the first Star Wars story ever told. It is known for being the perfect example of the Hero’s Journey.


Episode V – The Empire Strikes Back

After the damaged done by Luke Skywalker and the rest of the Rebels, the Empire doesn’t stop until the capture of Skywalker associates, Han Solo and Leah Organa. In order to safe his friends, Luke abandons his training with the Jedi Master Yoda in Dagobah and rushes to Bespin, the Cloud City where the rebels were captured by Darth Vader, the Emperor’s right hand. Luke loses his hand in combat with Vader, Leah hears Luke’s cry for help through the Force and saves him.
This movie is responsible for one of the most iconic plot twists in cinema History and it is well regarded as one of the best Star Wars movies.

Episode VI - Return of the Jedi

Luke continues and the team save Han from Jabba’s Palace in Tatooine. Luke tries to convince his father, Darth Vader, to abandon the Dark Side of the Force and join him as his former self, Anakin Skywalker, to face and defeat the Emperor.
The Rebels fight for Sanctuary, an Endor moon, with the objective of deactivate the Second Death Star’s shield and allow Lando Calrissian and Wedge Antilles to blow it up.
